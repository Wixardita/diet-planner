Manifesto

The future does not build new destinations. It unlocks forgotten ones.

For more than a century, roads defined real estate value. Personal air mobility may change that logic. Places once considered too remote can become private refuges of exceptional value — provided architecture, energy, access and regulation are treated with equal seriousness.

The Hidden Inventory

A fragmented archive of stone, altitude and abandonment.

Italy contains a vast hidden property inventory: ruderi, casali, abandoned hamlets, shelters and rural stone structures in positions where new construction would be difficult or impossible to authorise today.

Working assumption only: references to abandoned rural and mountain structures require validation during project development.

Before / After

Conservation first. Intervention second.

Original stone is preserved where technically feasible, then paired with contemporary openings, minimal interiors, solar and storage, water collection, smart home systems and a discreet landing area subject to approvals.
